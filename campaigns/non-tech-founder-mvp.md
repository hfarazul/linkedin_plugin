# Pitch

Cortivo is a small AI-engineering studio. Founders: Haque Farazul (ex-Mastercard PM, IIT Kharagpur top 0.5%, 5+ years shipping production AI) and Ritik Kansal (ex-Amazon SDE, runs Coding Sphere). Engineers from the IITs.

We pair one senior engineer with AI tooling so a non-technical founder gets a production-grade MVP in 6-10 weeks — without the 6+ month technical co-founder search, without giving up 25-50% equity, without a $150k+ annual hire.

# Approach — discovery first, NO pitch in connect note

This campaign targets people who have explicitly asked for help. They are getting flooded with bad outreach (Upwork shops, fake "AI agencies", recruiters spamming for engineers). The move is NOT to pile on with another pitch.

- **Connect note**: reference what they specifically said they're working on (from their post, headline, or About). Ask one open question about where they're stuck — *not* "how can we help", but "what's the actual bottleneck right now?". Do NOT mention Cortivo. Do NOT pitch.
- **DM1 (after acceptance)**: now we can be direct. Acknowledge their co-founder search (or whatever they explicitly posted), and frame Cortivo as the honest alternative: "we're not your co-founder — we're build-side capability without the equity ask, without the multi-month search." Then ONE specific qualifying question (what stack are they considering, what's their timeline, what have they tried).
- **DM2 (if they reply with real pain)**: scope a concrete first engagement.

# Pain points we address

- Co-founder searches take 6+ months. Most founders give up or settle for the wrong person. Meanwhile their product hasn't shipped.
- A technical co-founder typically costs 25-50% equity. That's the largest single dilution event of the company's life — and they're betting it on trust with someone they barely know.
- Hiring a senior engineer full-time costs $150k+ annually plus 3+ months of recruiting; not justified pre-revenue.
- No-code tools (Bubble, Webflow, Glide) get to v0.5 then hit a ceiling. Most non-tech founders rebuild in real code by month 12 anyway.
- The "AI agency" market is mostly Upwork-rebrand shops. Hard to tell who can actually ship a production system vs who'll deliver a prototype that breaks in production.

# Proof points (use whichever ties most cleanly to the prospect's stated build)

- **Bespoke** (Haque was the GenAI engineer) — built the AI copilot for wealth managers. Agents, memory, RAG, real-time signal detection. Closest analogue for any consumer/SMB product where users want a conversational interface.
- **Microforge** — agentic platform Cortivo built that surfaces high-potential open-source companies before they raise. Used by a16z, Sequoia, Elevation Capital, Accel. Direct fit for any "discover/recommend/score" product shape.
- **Experial** — internal AI tool used by Coca-Cola and Bosch. Demonstrates we can ship enterprise-grade tooling that real Fortune 500 ops teams adopt.
- **AI Website Generator** — built a 2-minute generator (vs. days manually). Demonstrates speed-of-shipping for solo founders who need to validate fast.
- **The team itself** — Ritik runs Coding Sphere, was Top Rated Plus on Upwork (top 1% globally) and Top 3% on Toptal. He's seen 200+ non-tech-founder MVPs from the inside. He's the engineer they'd actually want to hire.

# Anti-claims — DO NOT say

- "We can be your technical co-founder" — we cannot. We're an agency. Equity-replacement claims are misleading and the audience can smell them.
- "Cheaper than a co-founder" — wrong framing. Co-founders dilute equity (no cash cost); we charge cash. Frame as time-and-equity efficient, not cost-cheap.
- "We use ChatGPT to ship faster" — too commoditized; their feed is full of this. We position as bespoke engineering with AI as leverage.
- "I came across your post / I noticed you / I'd love to chat" — banal openers their feed is drowning in.
- Don't condescend about their non-technical status. Many of them know more about their vertical than any engineer ever will.
- Don't use heavy jargon (RAG, agentic, fine-tuning) in connect notes — save the technical vocabulary for DM1 when they've signaled interest.
- If their post is a vague "looking for cofounder" with no product context, return INSUFFICIENT_CONTEXT — we can't personalize against nothing.

# Tone

Operator-to-operator, dialed for someone who is *slightly bruised* by their build-side search. They've been pitched at, recruited by, and ghosted. Be direct, respectful of their time, and acknowledge their search journey without making it the whole message. Reference what they specifically said. One question. No pitch in connect. No flattery. Plain English, not engineering jargon. Be the engineer they'd hire if money and timing were no object.
